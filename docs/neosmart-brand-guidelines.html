Foundation
What the brand believes, who it's for, and how it behaves — before a single pixel.
The idea

One sentence the whole brand grows from.

We build the systems, products, and brands our clients need — around them, and theirs to keep.

Everything downstream — the voice, the type, the colour, the way a call is run — is this idea, expressed. The studio is not the subject. The client is. Neosmart's job is to build the thing itself, put the same hands on it start to finish, and hand it over working.

Positioning

Said plainly, from the client's side.

For founders and operators who want their systems, products, and brand built around their business and handed over as theirs to own — Neosmart puts the same hands on the work from first sketch to running result, and builds everything to keep working after it leaves.

The category sells throughput and speed. Neosmart gives each client the studio's full attention and leaves them owning the outcome. That difference is the whole position — and it's checkable, not claimed.

Audience

Who it's for — and who it isn't.

Primary — the client

Founders & operators who want to own what's built.

  • Funded startups and established businesses modernising.
  • Teams burned by handoffs, junior swaps, and dead deliverables.
  • People who want systems their own team runs — not a rented black box.
  • Buyers who read undivided attention as the real mark of quality.
Not for

The work we turn down, in our own words.

  • “We don't accept projects where design is treated as decoration.”
  • “We work with founders who value clarity over speed.”
  • Anyone shopping the cheapest quote across five vendors — we don't bid.
  • Staff augmentation — we don't rent hours.
Expression

Personality, defined by its guardrails.

Each trait is held in place by what it must never become. The guardrail is what keeps the brand from drifting into parody.

Devoted, not servile
Full attention, shown in the work — never flattery or fawning.
Exacting, not cold
Details in service of the client, never in service of superiority.
Quiet, not vague
Quiet about selling, precise about facts. Says less, means it.
Assured, not arrogant
Comfortable saying no — never a velvet rope or an exclusivity act.
Unhurried, not slow
Built to last, not shipped fast. A quality standard, not a pace complaint.
What's true for the client

The three things every engagement guarantees.

Built around you

We build around your business and how it actually works — you're the brief, not a template we fit you into.

The same hands, start to finish

The people who design your work build it and stay with it. Your contact is the person making the thing.

Yours to keep

Everything ends in something you own and run — a system, product, or brand that keeps working after we're gone.

The wordmark

A word, not a compound. One capital.

Neosmart is set as a single, composed word — never a mark, mascot, or gradient. The full stop is the one place the accent appears: a small punctuation of finality that says the work is finished.

The accent — locked

Oxblood is the accent — deep enough to read as considered, and deliberately not the bright terracotta that has become the default everywhere. Clay and ochre were considered and set aside. Only one accent exists, and it never appears in gradients.

Paper · ~70%Ink · ~25%~5%

Working ratio: paper dominates, ink structures, the accent is a sliver. Ink or paper appears on every surface; the accent never exceeds roughly one-twentieth of it. If the accent stops feeling special, there's too much of it.

Typography

One grotesk. Character from setting, not letterforms.

A single neutral grotesk across everything, separated by weight, scale, and tracking — no second face to pair. Recommended licensed face: Neue Haas Grotesk or Söhne. Shown here in Hanken Grotesk, the accessible fallback.

The measure. It measures — it doesn't decorate.

The refusal — a brand asset, published

“We don't accept projects where design is treated as decoration.”

“We work with founders who value clarity over speed.”

Write from the client's side of the screen — “yours,” “your product,” “built around you.” One call to action, said once, never chased. Claims checkable within one scroll. We never count clients or advertise scarcity; the care shows in the work, not in how exclusive it sounds.
